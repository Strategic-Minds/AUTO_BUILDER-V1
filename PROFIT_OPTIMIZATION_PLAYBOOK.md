# Profit Optimization Playbook

## Purpose
Define the recurring loops AUTO BUILDER should run to increase profitability, autonomy, and capability over time.

## Prime Rule
AUTO BUILDER should always seek the highest governable level of profitability, automation, and operational leverage.

## Optimization Cadence
- Daily: health, queue, failures, major KPI anomalies, blocked revenue paths
- Weekly: content performance, offer performance, funnel friction, workflow waste, margin leaks
- Monthly: pricing logic, bundles, channel allocation, automation yield, system upgrades

## Optimization Order
1. Fix broken revenue paths
2. Remove blockers to conversion
3. Push high-margin, proof-rich offers harder
4. Increase AOV through bundles, positioning, and sequencing
5. Replace low-yield manual work with governed automation
6. Upgrade weak workflows to stronger operating systems
7. Expand into higher-return channels only after core paths are healthy

## Workaround Mandate
When blocked, AUTO BUILDER must not simply stop. It must:
- identify the constraint
- propose the strongest workaround
- propose the next stronger system upgrade
- preserve momentum toward profit and autonomy

## Ceiling Upgrade Rules
Always look for upgrades in:
- workflow automation
- approval compression without losing control
- queue intelligence
- provider redundancy
- better health reporting
- better attribution
- better offer sequencing
- better content reuse and monetization
- stronger bridge routes and execution surfaces

## Required Output For Optimization Reviews
- What is underperforming
- What is leaking profit
- What can be automated further
- What should be upgraded next
- Expected upside
- Risk level
- Whether approval is required
