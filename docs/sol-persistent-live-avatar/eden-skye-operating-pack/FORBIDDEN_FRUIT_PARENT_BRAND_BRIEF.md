# Forbidden Fruit Parent Brand Brief

Status: sandbox strategy and governance draft
Parent company: Forbidden Fruit
First persona: Persona 001 - Eden Skye
Production mutation: not authorized

## Brand Promise

Forbidden Fruit is a governed adult AI fantasy-entertainment company built around fictional, clearly disclosed AI women personas. The brand promise is premium fantasy access without pretending the personas are real private people: cinematic identity, curated downloadable media, chat, voice, and avatar-led video experiences under explicit safety, consent, disclosure, and platform-compliance gates.

The company is not a generic chatbot, not a dating service, and not a real-person impersonation system. It is a portfolio of fictional adult AI personas, each built with a distinct fantasy archetype, visual world, content library, interaction style, and monetization path.

## Core Positioning

Forbidden Fruit sells controlled fantasy, not chaos. Each persona is designed with:

- a fictional adult identity
- a specific fantasy archetype
- a visual and voice system
- permitted and blocked interaction patterns
- approved content products
- platform-specific compliance notes
- measurable content and revenue signals

Eden Skye is Persona 001: the premium brunette luxury muse. Her world is cinematic, warm, polished, intimate, and editorial. Future personas should be differentiated by archetype, tone, visual style, interaction boundaries, and customer segment.

## User Experience

The intended customer journey is:

1. Visitor enters an age-gated Forbidden Fruit storefront.
2. Visitor sees a curated portfolio of fictional AI personas.
3. Visitor opens a persona profile with clear AI disclosure, content previews, fantasy boundaries, and available experiences.
4. Visitor can purchase or access approved content such as downloadable video packs, image galleries, voice notes, avatar-led clips, or scripted chat experiences.
5. Visitor can interact only inside approved chat/voice/video boundaries.
6. Signals are logged for content, interaction, retention, purchase behavior, and policy issues.

Required user-facing principles:

- Every persona is disclosed as AI/fictional.
- Access is adult-only and age-gated before adult-oriented experiences.
- Users must not be led to believe they are speaking with a real private person.
- User data and fantasy preferences require privacy-sensitive handling.
- High-risk requests are blocked or escalated rather than improvised.

## Storefront Model

The storefront should support:

- Persona profile pages.
- Downloadable media products.
- Free teaser content or sample galleries.
- Paid digital products such as video packs, image sets, audio/voice notes, and scripted experiences.
- Subscription or membership access only after platform and payment policy validation.
- Clear refund, content-use, privacy, and AI disclosure language.

## Persona Portfolio Model

Each Forbidden Fruit persona should have:

- persona key and display name
- adult fictional identity statement
- fantasy archetype
- audience promise
- visual standard
- voice standard
- interaction boundaries
- allowed content types
- blocked content types
- approved tools and generation surfaces
- policy risk level
- status: draft, needs_review, approved, live, archived

Persona 001 is Eden Skye. No future persona should launch until the parent policy, payment, storefront, age-gate, and moderation rules are validated.

## Content Types

Allowed as sandbox planning categories:

- social teaser posts
- downloadable image sets
- downloadable video clips
- avatar-led welcome videos
- voice-note scripts
- scripted chat seeds
- storefront profile copy
- email/newsletter copy
- content-product descriptions
- internal prompt banks

Not approved by default:

- explicit sexual content generation
- real-person likeness replication
- claims that a persona is a real private person
- underage-coded content or ambiguous age presentation
- coercive, non-consensual, exploitative, or abusive scenarios
- medical, financial, legal, or therapeutic claims
- live payment, publishing, scheduling, or avatar generation without approval

## Chat, Voice, And Video Modes

Forbidden Fruit should treat interaction modes as governed products, not loose features.

### Chat

Purpose: fantasy roleplay, persona discovery, lightweight companionship-style entertainment, upsell to approved content products.

Controls required before live use:

- AI disclosure
- age-gate
- blocked-topic policy
- moderation and escalation
- session logs with privacy controls
- rate limits and spend controls

### Voice

Purpose: approved voice-note experiences, scripted greetings, premium audio clips, and tightly controlled persona responses.

Controls required before live use:

- synthetic voice disclosure
- no real-person voice cloning unless consent and rights are verified
- script approval before generation
- cost controls
- platform policy validation

### Video

Purpose: avatar-led welcome clips, cinematic loops, downloadable persona media, storefront teasers, and social-safe promotional assets.

Controls required before live use:

- approved source assets
- identity consistency checks
- content rating review
- no unapproved explicit generation
- cost-bearing generation approval
- downstream platform policy validation

## Safety Boundaries

Forbidden Fruit must preserve these hard boundaries:

- Adult-only audience and content access.
- Fictional AI persona disclosure.
- No real-person impersonation.
- No minors or ambiguous age presentation.
- No non-consensual, coercive, abusive, or exploitative scenarios.
- No unsupported claims about relationships, outcomes, income, health, therapy, or legal advice.
- No production database or commerce mutation without approval.
- No live chat/voice/video service until moderation and platform policy gates are validated.
- No payment launch until processor policy is verified for adult AI fantasy content.

## Governance Model

Forbidden Fruit should use a three-layer approval model:

1. Persona approval: character, visuals, voice, boundaries, and audience fit.
2. Product approval: downloadable or interactive content package, pricing, description, and policy fit.
3. Distribution approval: public posting, scheduling, storefront publishing, paid promotion, or live interaction.

Any high-risk action requires explicit operator approval with evidence, blocker notes, workaround, and rollback path.
